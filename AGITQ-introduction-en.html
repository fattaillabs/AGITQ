Basic Concept

The AGITQ trading strategy uses the 200-day moving average of TQQQ (NASDAQ 3x Leveraged ETF) to assess market conditions and execute appropriate asset allocation.

Three Market Conditions & Action Rules

The core of this strategy lies in three clear action rules based on market conditions.

üî¥ Bearish Condition

TQQQ Price < 200MA

Sell all growth assets (TQQQ, S&P500 ETF) and convert to 100% SGOV (Short-term Treasury ETF).

üü¢ Concentrated Investment

200MA < TQQQ Price < 200MA + 5%

Convert all safe assets (SGOV) to TQQQ. New funds should also buy TQQQ. However, if you hold S&P500 ETF from the "Overheated" phase, keep holding it.

‚Üí This is the key zone for maximizing returns.

üü° Overheated Condition

TQQQ Price > 200MA + 5%

Hold existing TQQQ positions. New funds (salary, etc.) should buy S&P500 ETF to manage risk.

‚Üí This may signal short-term overheating, so add stable assets instead of chasing.

‚ö†Ô∏è Very Important: Why You Should Keep S&P500 ETF

During "Concentrated Investment," you might be tempted to sell S&P500 ETF and go all-in on TQQQ. However, this can be the worst decision that eats away at long-term returns.

S&P500 ETF acts as a 'ballast' that prevents your entire portfolio from collapsing when TQQQ crashes. This small stability creates huge return differences through the magic of compounding over the long term.

Of course, when "Bearish Condition" arrives, S&P500 ETF should also be sold according to the rules and converted to SGOV.
